— About

Nearly six years. Thousands of hours. One goal.

Jacob Robinson

Host, Law of Code. Dual J.D. in U.S. and Canada. Trained as a lawyer, now obsessed with building the world's best legal podcast.

I started the Law of Code podcast in law school because I needed an excuse to talk to lawyers about an emerging technology I was obsessed with: Bitcoin. Since then I've spoken with hundreds of lawyers regulators and entrepreneurs about emerging technology and the law.

After graduating I began articling at a major Canadian firm where I worked on what was at the time the largest crypto M&A transaction in Canadian history. While articling I was poached — which doesn't usually happen to someone with technically zero years of practice — to join one of Canada's top firms. In my first months as a first-year associate I helped land a client with a market cap of over a billion dollars and assisted major crypto exchanges in navigating the Canadian legal landscape. I kept the podcast going the whole time.

Six months into my legal career a tumor started growing. I didn't know it. I kept getting sicker for nearly two years before anyone found it. Then came chemo and radiation. I was 29.

That stretch gave me a new perspective on what I wanted to spend my time on. Not long after I finished radiation I realized the Law of Code podcast was what I truly loved doing — and that I wanted to build it into a career helping lawyers and operators understand the legal layer of emerging tech. I have been tumor free since the fall of 2025.

In April 2026 I decided to make Law of Code the best legal podcast in the world. Today it is the world's No. 1 legal tech podcast (if you disagree, tell me why and I'll improve it), with thousands of lawyers, regulators and builders listening. The plan from here is to keep going wider — into every major legal vertical — without ever giving up the depth.
